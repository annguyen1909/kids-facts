## What is a clownfish?

A clownfish is a **small, colorful reef fish** famous for living with sea anemones. Ocellaris clownfish have bright orange bodies with white bands and black outlines. They dart among anemone tentacles that would sting most other fish.

Ocellaris clownfish belong to the family Pomacentridae. Their scientific name is *Amphiprion ocellaris*.

## Where do clownfish live?

Ocellaris clownfish live on **warm coral reefs** in the Indo-Pacific Ocean. They are found from Australia and Indonesia to the Philippines and Japan.

Healthy reefs with living sea anemones give clownfish shelter, food, and safe places to lay eggs.

## What do clownfish eat?

Clownfish are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat algae, plankton, tiny crustaceans, and leftover bits of food near their anemone.

They often make short feeding trips away from the anemone, then hurry back to safety among the tentacles.

## How do clownfish live?

Clownfish usually live in a **small group** around one sea anemone. A dominant female leads the group, while males help guard eggs laid on nearby rock.

A special **mucus coating** helps clownfish avoid being stung by their host anemone.

## Why are clownfish at risk?

Ocellaris clownfish are listed as **Least Concern**, but they depend on healthy coral reefs and sea anemones. Reef damage, pollution, and warming ocean water can harm the habitats they need.

Protecting reefs helps clownfish, sea turtles, and many other ocean animals survive.

## Life cycle and larvae

Clownfish lay hundreds of tiny **eggs** on a clean rock near their anemone. The male guards the eggs and fans them until they hatch.

The young larvae drift in open water before settling on a reef and finding an anemone home of their own.

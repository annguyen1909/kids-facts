## What is a meerkat?

A **meerkat** is a small mongoose from southern Africa. Meerkats have slim bodies, dark eye patches, and a habit of standing upright to scan for danger.

Meerkats belong to the mongoose family Herpestidae. Their scientific name is *Suricata suricatta*.

## Where do meerkats live?

Meerkats live in **dry grasslands and deserts** of Botswana, Namibia, and South Africa. They dig burrow systems in hard, sandy soil.

Explore more animals from open grassland habitats on the [savanna trail](/habitats/savanna).

## What do meerkats eat?

Meerkats are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat insects, scorpions, spiders, small reptiles, eggs, and sometimes roots.

Sharp claws help them dig prey out of the ground quickly.

## How do meerkats live?

Meerkats live in [social groups](/topics/social-animals) called mobs or gangs. One meerkat often acts as a **sentry**, standing on hind legs while others forage.

Alarm calls warn the group when hawks, jackals, or snakes are nearby.

## Why are meerkats at risk?

Meerkats are listed as **Least Concern**, but drought and habitat clearing can reduce food and burrow sites in some regions.

Healthy dry grasslands with room to dig help meerkat groups survive.

## Life cycle and pups

A baby meerkat is called a **pup**. Pups are born underground in burrows where the group can protect them from heat and predators.

Young meerkats learn by watching adults how to dig, hunt, and respond to alarm calls.

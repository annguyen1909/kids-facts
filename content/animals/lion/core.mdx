## What is a lion?

A lion is one of the biggest wild cats in the world. Most lions live in **prides**—family groups that hunt, rest, and raise cubs together. That makes lions special: they are the only big cats that usually live in large groups.

Lions belong to the cat family [Felidae](/families/felidae). Their scientific name is *Panthera leo*.

## Where do lions live?

Lions live in open habitats across Africa—especially **savannas** and grasslands where they can spot prey and move in a pride. A small wild group also lives in India's Gir Forest.

Tall grass helps them hide. Seasonal water draws zebra, wildebeest, and antelope—the animals lions often hunt.

## What do lions eat?

Lions are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat meat from animals such as zebra, wildebeest, and antelope.

**Lionesses** usually hunt together. They stalk through cover, then sprint in a short, fast chase. Lions are powerful sprinters, not long-distance runners.

## How do lions live together?

A pride often includes related females, their cubs, and one or more adult males. Females usually stay in the pride they were born into. Males defend territory and protect the group.

Lions roar to warn rivals and stay in contact across open land. A roar can travel for miles on a quiet night. Read more about [social animals](/topics/social-animals).

## Why are lions at risk?

Adult lions have few natural enemies, but wild numbers are **falling**. Lions are listed as [Vulnerable](/conservation-status/vulnerable).

Habitat loss, fewer prey animals, and conflict with people are the biggest threats today. Protected parks and community programs help keep the last wild prides safe.

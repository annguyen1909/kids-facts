## What is a giant anteater?

A giant anteater is a large, shaggy **mammal** with a long, tube-shaped snout and a big bushy tail. It is the biggest of all the anteaters and can stretch about seven feet from its nose to the tip of its tail.

Even though it eats ants, the giant anteater is not related to bugs. It belongs to a group of mammals called **Pilosa**, the same group as sloths. Its scientific name is *Myrmecophaga tridactyla*, which means "ant-eater with three fingers."

## Where do giant anteaters live?

Giant anteaters live in **Central and South America**, from Honduras all the way south to northern Argentina. They roam open **grasslands** and tropical [savannas](/habitats/savanna), as well as wetlands and forests.

They especially like places with lots of **ant and termite nests** to raid. Big wild areas like Brazil's Pantanal wetlands and the dry Cerrado grasslands are important homes for them.

## What do giant anteaters eat?

Giant anteaters are **insectivores**, which means they eat insects. Their favorite foods are **ants** and **termites**, and they almost never eat anything else.

An anteater first rips open a nest with its strong front **claws**. Then it pokes in its narrow snout and flicks out its long, sticky tongue to catch the insects. It eats fast and moves on quickly, before the soldier ants and termites can bite back. One anteater can slurp up tens of thousands of insects in a single day.

## The amazing tongue and claws

A giant anteater has no teeth at all. Instead, it has a tongue that can be almost **two feet long**, longer than its own head. The tongue is covered in sticky spit and tiny hooks, and it can flick in and out about **150 times a minute**.

Its front feet have huge, curved **claws** for tearing open nests. The claws are so long that the anteater walks on its **knuckles** to keep them sharp. Those same claws are powerful weapons, and an anteater can use them to defend itself from a jaguar or puma.

## How do giant anteaters behave?

Giant anteaters mostly live **alone**. Each one wanders its own patch of land, sniffing out nests with a sense of smell about 40 times stronger than ours. Their eyesight is poor, so they rely on their nose.

They are usually quiet and slow-moving, but they can **gallop** away in short bursts if they are scared. The only lasting family bond is between a mother and her single pup.

## Life cycle and babies

A baby giant anteater is called a **pup**. A mother has just one pup at a time, after carrying it for about six months.

The newborn climbs onto its mother's back and **rides** there for many months while it grows. The pup's black-and-white stripe lines up perfectly with its mother's, so it is hard for a predator to tell where she ends and the baby begins. It stays with her until it is big enough to find food on its own.

## Why are giant anteaters at risk?

Giant anteaters are listed as [Vulnerable](/conservation-status/vulnerable), which means their numbers are slowly going down. They face several dangers from people.

The biggest problems are **habitat loss** when grasslands are turned into farms, grass fires that burn their homes, and being hit by cars on busy roads. Protecting wild grasslands and savannas helps keep these gentle, ant-loving animals safe.

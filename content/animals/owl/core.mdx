## What is an owl?

An owl is a **bird of prey** built for hunting at night. The great horned owl has a rounded face, fierce yellow eyes, and tall feather tufts often called "horns."

Great horned owls live across the Americas. Their scientific name is *Bubo virginianus*.

## Where do owls live?

Great horned owls are **highly adaptable**. They live in forests, deserts, grasslands, wetlands, and even city parks from Canada and Alaska to South America.

They nest in tree hollows, on cliff ledges, and sometimes in old nests built by hawks or crows.

## What do owls eat?

Owls are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). Great horned owls catch rabbits, rats, mice, birds, and other small animals.

They hunt from a perch, listen for movement, then swoop down with **silent wings** and grab prey in powerful talons.

## How do owls live?

Owls are most active at **night**. A pair may stay together through the breeding season, hooting to mark territory and warn rivals.

Outside the nesting season, great horned owls often hunt alone across a large home range.

## Why are owls at risk?

Great horned owls are listed as **Least Concern** and remain widespread across the Americas. Still, they face local dangers such as car strikes, rodent poison, and loss of old trees for nesting.

Keeping forests and nest sites protected helps owls thrive near both wild land and towns.

## Life cycle and chicks

A baby owl is called a **chick** or owlet. Chicks hatch blind and covered in fluffy down. Parents bring food to the nest while the young grow flight feathers.

Fledglings practice wing flaps and short hops on branches before taking their first real flights.

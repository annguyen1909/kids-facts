## What is a platypus?

A **platypus** is one of the strangest mammals on Earth. It has a duck-like bill, webbed feet, waterproof fur, and a beaver-like tail. Platypuses swim well and hunt underwater in rivers.

Platypuses belong to the monotreme group of egg-laying mammals. Their scientific name is *Ornithorhynchus anatinus*.

## Where do platypuses live?

Platypuses live along **freshwater rivers, streams, and lakes** in eastern Australia and Tasmania. They need clean water, muddy banks, and burrows for shelter.

Quiet forest rivers with healthy banks are some of the best platypus habitat.

## What do platypuses eat?

Platypuses are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They dive underwater to hunt insect larvae, worms, shrimp, crayfish, and snails in river mud.

They store food in cheek pouches and eat at the surface after a dive.

## How do platypuses live?

Platypuses spend much of their time **alone** in the water or in burrows along riverbanks. They are often active in the early morning and evening.

Females lay one to three eggs and raise **puggles** in a protected burrow after they hatch.

## Why are platypuses at risk?

Platypuses are listed as **Near Threatened**. Damaged riverbanks, pollution, drought, and land clearing can make life harder for wild platypuses.

Protecting clean freshwater habitat helps platypuses and many other river animals survive.

## Life cycle and puggles

A baby platypus is called a **puggle**. Females lay soft eggs in a burrow. After hatching, puggles nurse from milk patches on the mother's belly.

Young platypuses learn to hunt underwater as they grow and leave the burrow.

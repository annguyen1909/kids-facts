## What is a raccoon?

A **raccoon** is a clever mammal with a black mask across its eyes and a bushy ringed tail. Raccoons are famous for using their front paws like hands.

Common raccoons belong to the family Procyonidae. Their scientific name is *Procyon lotor*.

## Where do raccoons live?

Raccoons live in **forests, wetlands, and cities** across North America. They are also found in parts of Europe and Japan where people introduced them.

They often make dens in hollow trees, burrows, or attics.

## What do raccoons eat?

Raccoons are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore) that eat almost anything. They enjoy fruit, nuts, insects, fish, eggs, and food scraps near people.

They often dip food in water before eating, which is how they got their name.

## How do raccoons live?

Raccoons are **nocturnal**, meaning they are most active at night. They climb trees, swim well, and explore neighborhoods looking for meals.

A mother raccoon raises her **kits** and teaches them how to find food.

## Why are raccoons at risk?

Common raccoons are listed as **Least Concern** and often do well near people. In places where they were introduced, they can harm native wildlife.

Keeping trash secured and giving wild raccoons space helps people and raccoons live safely together.

## Life cycle and kits

Baby raccoons are called **kits**. They are born in spring, usually in litters of two to five. Kits open their eyes after a few weeks and soon follow their mother on night walks.

By fall, young raccoons learn to find food on their own.

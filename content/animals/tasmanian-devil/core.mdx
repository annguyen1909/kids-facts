## What is a Tasmanian devil?

The Tasmanian devil is the world's largest [carnivorous](/diets/carnivore) marsupial—a stocky, black-furred animal about the size of a small dog. It carries its young in a pouch, like a kangaroo, but hunts and scavenges meat with remarkable ferocity.

Its scientific name is *Sarcophilus harrisii*, which literally means "Harris's flesh-lover." That name suits it well.

## Where do Tasmanian devils live?

Tasmanian devils are found only on **Tasmania**, the island state off the south coast of Australia. They once roamed mainland Australia but vanished there several thousand years ago, likely due to competition with dingoes.

They live across a variety of habitats on Tasmania—from coastal scrubland and open grassland to dense [temperate forest](/habitats/temperate-forest). They shelter in hollow logs, caves, and thick vegetation during the day.

## What do Tasmanian devils eat?

Tasmanian devils are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore) that eat wombats, wallabies, birds, reptiles, and carrion. They are powerful scavengers and will consume an entire carcass—**bones, fur, and organs included**—leaving almost nothing behind.

This complete consumption is actually valuable to the ecosystem. It removes rotting meat and reduces the spread of disease in the bush. Explore more [Australian animals](/topics/australian-animals).

## How do Tasmanian devils behave?

Tasmanian devils are mostly **solitary and nocturnal**. They forage alone at night, traveling several miles in search of food. When multiple devils meet at a carcass, they produce loud screeches, growls, and coughs that carry far through the night.

These dramatic vocalizations—combined with their wide-gaping yawn—help establish dominance without serious fighting. Their thick tail stores fat reserves for lean times.

## Life cycle and young

A female gives birth after just 21 days of pregnancy. Newborn joeys are **no bigger than a grain of rice** and must crawl unaided into the mother's backward-facing pouch. She has only four nipples, so only four joeys can survive even from a litter of up to 30.

Surviving joeys stay in the pouch for about four months, then cling to the mother's back until they are independent at around nine months. Read more about [marsupials](/topics/marsupials).

## How powerful is a Tasmanian devil?

Despite its small size, the Tasmanian devil has the **strongest bite relative to body size** of any living mammal. Massive jaw muscles and a wide gape let it crush through bone that most predators cannot break.

A single devil can consume up to 40 percent of its own body weight in one feeding session—an extraordinary feat of metabolic efficiency.

## Why are Tasmanian devils endangered?

Tasmanian devils are listed as [Endangered](/conservation-status/endangered). A contagious cancer called **Devil Facial Tumor Disease (DFTD)** has killed more than 80 percent of the wild population since the 1990s. The tumors spread through biting during feeding and mating, preventing animals from eating.

Conservation programs breed disease-free devils in captivity and on predator-free islands as insurance populations. Researchers are also developing vaccines in hopes of protecting wild devils from this devastating disease.

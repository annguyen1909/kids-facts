## What is a wolf?

A wolf is a **wild dog** and one of the top predators in many northern ecosystems. It has long legs, a thick coat, sharp teeth, and a bushy tail.

Wolves belong to the dog family Canidae. Their scientific name is *Canis lupus*. Domestic dogs are very distant relatives of wolves.

## Where do wolves live?

Wolves once roamed much of the Northern Hemisphere. Today they live in **forests, mountains, tundra, and grasslands** across parts of North America, Europe, and Asia.

Healthy wolf populations need **large wild areas** with plenty of prey, water, and space for pack territories.

## What do wolves eat?

Wolves are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They hunt deer, elk, moose, bison, and other large animals. A pack may chase prey together or ambush animals in cover.

After a big kill, wolves can eat a large meal and then rest for days before hunting again.

## How do wolves live?

Wolves are famous for living in **packs**, often led by a breeding pair and their grown offspring. Pack members [travel and hunt together](/topics/social-animals), share food, and defend territory.

Wolves also **howl**, scent-mark, and use body language to communicate.

## Why are wolves at risk?

Gray wolves are listed as **Least Concern** worldwide, but history tells a harder story. People once hunted wolves heavily, and many regions lost them completely.

Today, protected areas and reintroduction programs help wolves return to some wild landscapes.

## Life cycle and pups

A baby wolf is called a **pup**. Pups are born in spring inside a den. They are blind and helpless at first, then learn to hunt and travel with the pack over their first year.

Both parents and older siblings often help feed and protect the pups.

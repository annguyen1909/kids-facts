## What is a chameleon?

A **chameleon** is a lizard famous for its swiveling eyes, gripping feet, and long sticky tongue. Veiled chameleons have a tall casque on the head and often show bright green colors.

Veiled chameleons belong to the family Chamaeleonidae. Their scientific name is *Chamaeleo calyptratus*.

## Where do chameleons live?

Veiled chameleons live in **trees and bushes** in dry mountain regions of the Arabian Peninsula, mainly Yemen and Saudi Arabia. They spend most of their time climbing branches.

Their feet and tail help them hold tightly to twigs and leaves.

## What do chameleons eat?

Chameleons are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat insects, spiders, and other small animals. They hunt by shooting out a **sticky tongue** faster than the eye can follow.

Because they move slowly, surprise tongue strikes are their main hunting tool.

## How do chameleons live?

Chameleons usually live **alone** and move slowly through the branches. They change color with mood, temperature, and light.

Each eye can look in a different direction, which helps them watch for prey and predators at the same time.

## Why are chameleons at risk?

Veiled chameleons are listed as **Least Concern**, but wild populations face pressure from habitat loss and collection for the pet trade.

Protecting dry forest and scrubland gives chameleons places to feed and lay eggs.

## Life cycle and hatchlings

A baby chameleon is called a **hatchling**. Females lay dozens of eggs in soil or leaf litter. After a long incubation, tiny hatchlings dig out and begin climbing on their own.

Young chameleons must learn quickly how to hunt insects in the trees.

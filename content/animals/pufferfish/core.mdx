## What is a pufferfish?

A **pufferfish** is a reef fish with a rounded body, beak-like teeth, and a famous defense trick—it can puff up like a spiky balloon. Guinea fowl pufferfish have dark bodies with white spots.

Guinea fowl pufferfish belong to the family Tetraodontidae. Their scientific name is *Arothron meleagris*.

## Where do pufferfish live?

Guinea fowl pufferfish live on **coral reefs** in the Pacific and Indian oceans. They are found from Mexico to the Galápagos and across tropical reef regions.

Healthy reefs give them food, shelter, and places to hide from predators.

## What do pufferfish eat?

Pufferfish are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat algae, sponges, and small invertebrates on the reef. Their strong jaws help them bite through tough foods.

They often search slowly among coral and rocks for meals.

## How do pufferfish live?

Pufferfish usually swim slowly and live **alone** on a reef home range. When threatened, they may **inflate with water** to look too big to swallow.

Some can also change between darker and lighter color patterns.

## Why are pufferfish at risk?

Guinea fowl pufferfish are listed as **Least Concern**, but they depend on healthy coral reefs. Reef damage, warming oceans, and pollution harm reef fish everywhere.

Protecting reefs helps pufferfish, clownfish, and countless ocean species.

## Life cycle and larvae

Young pufferfish begin life as tiny **larvae** drifting in open water. After growing, they settle on a reef and learn to find food and shelter.

Their toxins develop as they mature, making adults dangerous to many predators.

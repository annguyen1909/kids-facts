## What is a Komodo dragon?

A Komodo dragon is the **largest living lizard** on Earth. It has a long tail, tough scaly skin, sharp claws, and a forked yellow tongue that it uses to smell the air.

Komodo dragons belong to the monitor lizard family Varanidae. Their scientific name is *Varanus komodoensis*.

## Where do Komodo dragons live?

Wild Komodo dragons live only on a few **Indonesian islands**, including Komodo, Rinca, Flores, and Gili Motang. They use dry tropical forest, open grassland, and coastal scrub where they can bask in the sun.

Most of their remaining habitat is protected inside **Komodo National Park**.

## What do Komodo dragons eat?

Komodo dragons are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They hunt deer, wild pigs, water buffalo, and smaller animals. They also eat **carrion**—the remains of animals that have already died.

A Komodo dragon often waits quietly, then attacks with a fast bite. Its saliva contains harmful bacteria and venom that weaken prey.

## How do Komodo dragons behave?

Adult Komodo dragons usually live **alone** and defend feeding areas. Still, several dragons may gather when a large carcass provides enough food for more than one animal.

They spend time **basking** in the sun to warm up, then become more active while hunting. Their excellent sense of smell helps them find food from far away.

## Why are Komodo dragons at risk?

Komodo dragons are listed as **Endangered**. Their small island range, habitat loss, and fewer prey animals put wild populations under pressure.

National park protection, ranger patrols, and careful tourism rules help guard the dragons that still live in the wild.

## Life cycle and hatchlings

A baby Komodo dragon is called a **hatchling**. Females lay eggs in burrows or mound nests, and the eggs incubate for about seven to eight months before hatching.

Young hatchlings are vulnerable, so they often **climb trees** and eat insects and small prey until they grow large enough to live safely on the ground.

## What is a kangaroo?

A kangaroo is a **marsupial mammal** from Australia famous for hopping on its strong hind legs. The red kangaroo is the largest kangaroo species and has a long tail, big feet, and a front pouch.

Red kangaroos belong to the macropod family. Their scientific name is *Macropus rufus*.

## Where do kangaroos live?

Red kangaroos live across **dry inland Australia** in grasslands, scrub, and open woodlands. They are well suited to hot, open country where they can see predators from far away.

Explore more animals from open grassland habitats on the [savanna trail](/habitats/savanna).

## What do kangaroos eat?

Kangaroos are **herbivores**. They graze on grasses, leaves, and shrubs, often feeding in the cooler hours at dawn and dusk.

Their teeth and digestive system are adapted to tough, dry plants.

## How do kangaroos live?

Kangaroos often gather in loose groups called **mobs**, especially where food and water are reliable. Males may box or wrestle with their front legs to compete for status.

A kangaroo's powerful hop is one of the most efficient ways to travel quickly on land.

## Why are kangaroos at risk?

Red kangaroos are listed as **Least Concern** and remain widespread in Australia. Still, drought, habitat clearing, and road traffic can harm local populations.

Protected reserves and careful land management help kangaroos survive in changing landscapes.

## Life cycle and joeys

A baby kangaroo is called a **joey**. After a very short pregnancy, the tiny newborn crawls into the mother's **pouch**, where it nurses and grows for months.

Later, the joey begins peeking out, hopping nearby, and learning to graze with the mob.

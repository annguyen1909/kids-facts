## What is a peregrine falcon?

The peregrine falcon is a large, powerful bird of prey belonging to the falcon family Falconidae. They are famous for being the **fastest animals in the world**. 

Peregrines are about the size of a crow. They have a dark slate-blue back, barred white underparts, a black head, and a dark stripe down the side of their face that looks like a **mustache or helmet**.

Their scientific name is *Falco peregrinus*, which means \"wandering falcon.\" They are built for extreme speed, with long, pointed wings and powerful chest muscles that make them fly like fighter jets.

## Where do peregrine falcons live?

Peregrine falcons live in almost every part of the world, ranging from the cold Arctic tundra to hot tropical deserts. They are highly adaptable and inhabit **cliffs, mountains, river valleys, and coastlines**.

They love high places where they can spot prey easily. In recent years, peregrines have also adapted to **urban environments**. They nest on the ledges of skyscrapers, towers, and large bridges in major cities, where they hunt city pigeons.

## What do peregrine falcons eat?

Peregrine falcons are strict [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They feed almost exclusively on **medium-sized birds**, such as pigeons, doves, songbirds, starlings, and waterfowl. Sometimes, they will also hunt bats at dusk.

Their hunting style is spectacular. A falcon climbs high into the air, spots a bird flying below, and folds its wings to perform a vertical dive called a **stoop**. 

Diving down at speeds of over **200 miles per hour (320 km/h)**, the falcon strikes the prey mid-air with its feet, clutching it with sharp talons.

## How do peregrine falcons behave?

Peregrine falcons are solitary, highly territorial birds of prey. They mate for life and are extremely loyal to their nests, which are called **eyries**. An eyrie is usually a simple scrape made in gravel on a high cliff ledge.

Falcons communicate with loud, harsh calls that sound like *kak-kak-kak* to defend their territory. They will fiercely chase away larger birds, including eagles and owls, that get too close to their nesting sites.

To protect their eyes during super-fast dives, they have a translucent third eyelid called a **nictitating membrane** that sweeps across the eye to clear away dust.

## Life cycle and chicks

A baby falcon is called an **eyas**. The female falcon lays three to four reddish-brown eggs in the spring. Both the mother and father take turns keeping the eggs warm for about a month.

When the chicks hatch, they are covered in **soft, white down feathers** and are completely dependent on their parents for food. The parents bring them fresh meat, and the chicks grow rapidly. 

After about 40 days, the young falcons are fully feathered and begin learning to fly and hunt. They stay with their parents for a few more weeks before going off on their own.

## Why are peregrine falcons at risk?

Today, peregrine falcons are listed as Least Concern, but they have a famous history of survival. In the mid-1900s, they almost became extinct in North America and Europe due to the use of a chemical pesticide called **DDT**. 

DDT caused falcon eggs to have very thin shells that broke easily when the parents sat on them. 

Fortunately, after DDT was banned, conservation programs bred falcons in captivity and released them back into the wild. Their numbers recovered spectacularly, making the peregrine falcon one of the greatest conservation success stories in history.

## What is a wildebeest?

A **wildebeest** is a sturdy African antelope with a beard-like mane and curved horns. Blue wildebeest are famous for traveling in huge herds across the savanna.

Blue wildebeest belong to the family Bovidae. Their scientific name is *Connochaetes taurinus*.

## Where do wildebeest live?

Blue wildebeest live on **African savannas and grasslands** in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, Botswana, and South Africa. They need open plains with grass and reliable water.

The Serengeti-Mara ecosystem supports one of the greatest wildlife migrations on Earth.

## What do wildebeest eat?

Wildebeest are **herbivores** that graze on short grasses. They follow seasonal rains to find fresh green pasture.

Because they need water often, herds move toward rivers and lakes during dry months.

## How do wildebeest live?

Wildebeest are **herd animals** that travel, graze, and rest together. Being in a huge group helps confuse predators like lions and hyenas.

Males may fight with their horns during the breeding season.

## Why are wildebeest at risk?

Blue wildebeest are listed as **Least Concern**, but huge migrations depend on open routes between grasslands and water. Fences, farms, and drought can block these paths.

Protecting connected savanna habitat keeps migration alive.

## Life cycle and calves

Most wildebeest **calves** are born during the same few weeks each year. A newborn can stand and run within minutes.

Calves stay close to their mothers as the herd moves across the plains.

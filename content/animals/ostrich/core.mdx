## What is an ostrich?

An **ostrich** is the biggest bird in the world. It has a long neck, huge eyes, and powerful legs built for running instead of flying.

Common ostriches belong to the family Struthionidae. Their scientific name is *Struthio camelus*.

## Where do ostriches live?

Ostriches live on **dry grasslands and savannas** in Africa. They need open country where they can see predators from far away.

They are found from the Sahel region south to South Africa.

## What do ostriches eat?

Ostriches eat **plants, seeds, and insects**. They peck food from the ground and swallow small stones that help grind food in their stomach.

They can go without water for days by getting moisture from plants.

## How do ostriches live?

Ostriches live in small groups and are famous for **sprinting** away from danger. If cornered, they can deliver a powerful kick.

Females lay huge eggs in a ground nest, and parents guard the **chicks** together.

## Why are ostriches at risk?

Common ostriches are listed as **Least Concern**, but fencing, farming, and habitat loss have reduced wild numbers in some places.

Protecting open grasslands helps ostriches and other savanna wildlife.

## Life cycle and chicks

Ostrich eggs are the **largest of any bird**. After about six weeks, chicks hatch with striped down that helps them hide in grass.

Chicks can run within days and learn to feed with their parents.

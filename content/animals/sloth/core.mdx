## What is a sloth?

A **sloth** is a slow-moving mammal that lives high in rainforest trees. Brown-throated three-toed sloths have shaggy fur, dark eye masks, and long curved claws for hanging upside down.

Sloths belong to the family Bradypodidae. Their scientific name is *Bradypus variegatus*.

## Where do sloths live?

Brown-throated sloths live in **tropical rainforests** from Honduras through northern South America. They rarely come to the ground and spend nearly all their time in the canopy.

Tall forest trees give sloths leaves to eat and branches where they can rest hidden from predators.

## What do sloths eat?

Sloths are **herbivores**. They eat leaves, buds, and tender shoots from rainforest trees.

Because leaves are low in energy, sloths move slowly and rest for many hours each day.

## How do sloths live?

Sloths usually live **alone** in the trees. They climb slowly, hang upside down, and sleep for long stretches between meals.

Mother sloths carry **infants** on their bellies while moving through the canopy.

## Why are sloths at risk?

Brown-throated sloths are listed as **Least Concern**, but forest clearing and roads can still harm them. Slow movement makes crossing open ground dangerous.

Protecting connected rainforest gives sloths safer routes through the canopy.

## Life cycle and infants

A baby sloth is called an **infant**. After about six months of development, infants are born and cling tightly to their mothers.

Young sloths learn which leaves are safe to eat by watching their mothers in the trees.

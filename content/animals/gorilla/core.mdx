## What is a gorilla?

A gorilla is a **large ape** and one of the strongest plant-eating mammals in African forests. Gorillas have long arms, broad chests, and dark fur. Adult males often develop a silver stripe of hair on their backs and are called **silverbacks**.

Gorillas belong to the great ape family Hominidae. Their scientific name is *Gorilla gorilla*.

## Where do gorillas live?

Western lowland gorillas live in **tropical forests and swampy lowlands** in central and western Africa. They need thick forest cover, plenty of food plants, and quiet places to nest and raise infants.

Most wild gorillas survive inside **protected forests and national parks**.

## What do gorillas eat?

Gorillas are **herbivores**. They spend much of the day eating leaves, stems, fruit, shoots, and other forest plants.

A large adult male may eat more than 30 pounds of vegetation in a day while moving through the forest with its troop.

## How do gorillas live?

Gorillas live in [social troops](/topics/social-animals) usually led by one **silverback** male. Females and young gorillas stay close together, groom one another, and share forest trails.

When threatened, a silverback may beat his chest, roar, or charge to protect the group.

## Why are gorillas at risk?

Gorillas are listed as **Critically Endangered**. Their forests are being cut for farming and logging, and diseases such as Ebola have killed many wild gorillas.

Hunting and trapping also put pressure on remaining populations. Protected parks and anti-poaching patrols help gorillas survive.

## Life cycle and infants

A baby gorilla is called an **infant**. Infants are born after about eight and a half months and stay with their mothers for years.

Young gorillas learn by watching adults how to find food, climb, and behave in the troop.

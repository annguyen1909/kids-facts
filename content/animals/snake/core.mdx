## What is a ball python?

A **ball python** is a non-venomous snake from Africa with a thick body and beautiful brown-and-gold pattern. When frightened, it curls into a tight ball with its head tucked safely inside.

Ball pythons belong to the family Pythonidae. Their scientific name is *Python regius*.

## Where do ball pythons live?

Ball pythons live in **grasslands, savannas, and forest edges** in West and Central Africa. They spend much of the day hidden in burrows, termite mounds, or leaf litter.

Explore more animals from open grassland habitats on the [savanna trail](/habitats/savanna).

## What do ball pythons eat?

Ball pythons are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat rodents, small birds, and other small mammals. A python **swallows prey whole** and may not eat again for weeks after a large meal.

They hunt mostly at night using smell and heat-sensitive pits near the mouth.

## How do ball pythons live?

Ball pythons usually live **alone** and move quietly through their habitat. Females lay eggs and coil around them, sometimes **shivering their muscles** to keep the eggs warm.

They shed their skin as they grow, leaving behind an empty skin shell.

## Why are ball pythons at risk?

Ball pythons are listed as **Near Threatened**. Habitat loss and trapping for the pet trade have reduced some wild populations.

Protected savanna and careful trade rules help ball pythons survive in the wild.

## Life cycle and hatchlings

A baby snake is called a **hatchling**. Ball pythons lay a small clutch of eggs on the ground. After hatching, young snakes can hunt small prey on their own.

Hatchlings look like miniature adults but grow steadily with each shed.

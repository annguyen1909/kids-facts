## What is a Hercules beetle?

A Hercules beetle is a giant **rhinoceros beetle** from the tropical Americas. It is famous for its long body and for the huge horns carried by adult males.

Its scientific name is *Dynastes hercules*. Like other insects, it has six legs, a hard outer skeleton, and wing covers called **elytra** that protect the flying wings underneath.

## Where do Hercules beetles live?

Hercules beetles live in **humid tropical forests** from southern Mexico through Central America and into parts of South America and the Caribbean. They do best in warm places with lots of moisture, rotting wood, and fallen fruit.

During the day they often stay hidden in leaf litter, hollow logs, or tree crevices. At night they climb and fly in search of food and mates.

## What do Hercules beetles eat?

Adult Hercules beetles are [herbivores](/diets/herbivore). They mostly feed on **rotting fruit** and sometimes sip tree sap.

Their larvae eat something different. The grubs live in decaying wood and feed on soft, rotting plant material as they grow.

## Why do males have huge horns?

Male Hercules beetles use their horns to **wrestle rival males**. A beetle tries to lift and throw the other away from a food source or a female.

Females do not have the giant horns. They are usually shorter and sturdier, which helps them move through wood and soil while laying eggs.

## Life cycle and grubs

A female lays eggs in **rotting wood**. The eggs hatch into pale, C-shaped larvae called **grubs**. This is the longest stage of the beetle's life and can last many months.

When the grub is fully grown, it forms a chamber and changes into a pupa. After that, the adult beetle emerges with wings and, in males, its famous horns.

## Are Hercules beetles dangerous?

Hercules beetles may look fierce, but they are **not dangerous to people**. They do not sting, and they use their horns to battle other beetles, not to attack humans.

Their main defenses are their size, hard body, and ability to hide in the forest.

## Why are Hercules beetles at risk?

Hercules beetles are not usually treated as one of the most threatened insects, but they still depend on healthy **rainforest habitat**. When forests are cut down, they lose the rotting wood and food sources their young need.

Collecting beetles for trade can also put pressure on local populations. Protecting tropical forests helps these giant beetles survive.

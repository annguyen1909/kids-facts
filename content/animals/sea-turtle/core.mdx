## What is a sea turtle?

A sea turtle is a **reptile** that spends almost its whole life in the ocean. Green sea turtles have paddle-shaped front flippers, a streamlined shell, and a beak-like mouth for grazing plants.

Green sea turtles belong to the family Cheloniidae. Their scientific name is *Chelonia mydas*.

## Where do sea turtles live?

Green sea turtles live in **warm coastal oceans** around the world. They use coral reefs, bays, and **seagrass meadows** for feeding and shelter.

Adult females return to sandy beaches to lay eggs, often traveling long distances between feeding and nesting areas.

## What do sea turtles eat?

Adult green sea turtles are mostly **herbivores**. They eat seagrass and algae, helping keep underwater meadows healthy.

Young turtles may eat more small animals such as jellyfish and invertebrates before shifting to a plant-based diet as adults.

## How do sea turtles live?

Sea turtles usually live **alone**, though many may gather where food is abundant. They are strong swimmers and can migrate across entire ocean basins.

Green sea turtles sometimes rest on the seafloor or bask near the surface between feeding trips.

## Why are sea turtles at risk?

Green sea turtles are listed as **Endangered**. They face threats from fishing nets, plastic pollution, boat strikes, and loss of nesting beaches.

Protecting nesting beaches and reducing ocean pollution give hatchlings a better chance to survive.

## Life cycle and hatchlings

A baby sea turtle is called a **hatchling**. Females lay many eggs in a sandy nest. After incubation, tiny hatchlings dig out and scramble toward the ocean.

Only a small number survive to adulthood, but those that do may live for many decades.

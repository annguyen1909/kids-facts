## What is an octopus?

An **octopus** is a soft-bodied sea animal with eight arms, large eyes, and amazing camouflage skills. The common octopus is one of the smartest animals in the ocean.

Common octopuses belong to the family Octopodidae. Their scientific name is *Octopus vulgaris*.

## Where do octopuses live?

Common octopuses live in **coastal seas** of the eastern Atlantic and Mediterranean. They hide in rocky reefs, caves, and seagrass beds.

They often make dens under stones or inside empty shells.

## What do octopuses eat?

Octopuses are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They eat crabs, shrimp, clams, and fish. They sneak up on prey or burst from hiding to grab it with their arms.

Their beak can crack shells, and venom helps subdue prey.

## How do octopuses live?

Octopuses are **solitary hunters** that spend much of their time hiding. They can change color and skin texture in seconds to blend into rocks, sand, or algae.

If threatened, they may shoot an ink cloud and jet away.

## Why are octopuses at risk?

Common octopuses are listed as **Least Concern**, but pollution and heavy fishing affect coastal seas. Habitat loss and trawling can reduce places where octopuses find food and shelter.

Cleaner oceans and responsible fishing help protect them.

## Life cycle and hatchlings

Female octopuses lay thousands of eggs and guard them for weeks without eating. When the eggs hatch, tiny **hatchlings** drift in the ocean before settling on the seafloor.

Most octopuses live only one to two years.

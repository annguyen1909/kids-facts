## What is a moose?

A **moose** is the largest member of the deer family. Bulls grow enormous antlers, and all moose have long legs, a droopy nose, and a tall shoulder hump.

Moose belong to the family Cervidae. Their scientific name is *Alces alces*.

## Where do moose live?

Moose live in **northern forests and wetlands** across Canada, Alaska, Scandinavia, Russia, and parts of Europe and Asia. They need cool climates with lakes, rivers, and woody plants.

In summer, moose often wade into water to escape insects and heat.

## What do moose eat?

Moose are herbivores that browse on **leaves, twigs, bark, and aquatic plants**. They may eat more than 50 pounds of vegetation in a day.

Their long legs let them reach high branches that smaller animals cannot.

## How do moose live?

Moose are mostly **solitary** animals. Bulls grow new antlers each spring and use them in fall fights during the mating season.

Cows raise one or two **calves** and defend them fiercely from wolves and bears.

## Why are moose at risk?

Moose are listed as **Least Concern**, but some southern populations have declined because of warming winters, parasites, habitat loss, and car collisions.

Protecting forests and wetlands helps moose find food and shelter year-round.

## Life cycle and calves

Moose calves are born in **spring** after about eight months of development. They can stand and walk within hours and grow quickly on rich milk.

Young moose usually stay with their mother for about a year.

## What is a manta ray?

A giant oceanic manta ray is the **largest ray in the world**. Its broad fins look like wings, so it seems to fly through the sea.

Its scientific name is *Mobula birostris*. Manta rays are fish related to sharks, and their skeleton is made of flexible **cartilage** instead of bone.

## Where do manta rays live?

Manta rays live in **warm oceans** around the world. They travel through offshore waters but also visit coastal reefs, islands, and seamounts where food is plentiful.

They are strong long-distance swimmers and may move between feeding grounds, cleaning stations, and breeding areas.

## What do manta rays eat?

Manta rays are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore), but they eat very tiny prey. They feed on **zooplankton, krill, and tiny fish** by filtering seawater through their gills.

The two flaps beside the mouth, called **cephalic lobes**, help guide food-rich water inward while the ray swims.

## How do manta rays behave?

Manta rays often swim alone, but they may gather where plankton is thick or where small reef fish clean parasites from their skin. These places are called **cleaning stations**.

They are also known for **barrel-rolling** while feeding and for breaching high above the water's surface.

## Life cycle and pups

Manta rays give birth to live young. A baby manta ray is called a **pup**, and the mother usually has only **one pup at a time**.

That slow reproduction is important. Because manta rays do not have many babies, populations recover slowly after losses.

## Are manta rays dangerous?

No. Manta rays do not have a stinging tail spine, and they are not considered dangerous to people.

Their size can be impressive, but they are gentle filter feeders rather than hunters of large animals.

## Why are manta rays at risk?

Giant oceanic manta rays are listed as **Endangered**. They face threats from targeted fishing, accidental capture in nets, boat strikes, and pollution.

Protecting migration routes, feeding sites, and reef habitats is important because these rays grow slowly and reproduce slowly.

## What is a koala?

A koala is a **tree-dwelling marsupial** from Australia. Koalas have fluffy ears, a large nose, and strong claws for climbing. They are famous for resting in eucalyptus trees and eating leaves almost all day.

Koalas belong to the marsupial family Phascolarctidae. Their scientific name is *Phascolarctos cinereus*. Despite the nickname "koala bear," they are **not bears**.

## Where do koalas live?

Koalas live in **eucalyptus forests and woodlands** along Australia's eastern and southeastern coast. They need healthy gum trees for food and tall branches for safe resting spots.

Most wild koalas survive best where forest patches connect and give them room to move between feeding trees.

## What do koalas eat?

Koalas are **herbivores** that eat almost only **eucalyptus leaves**. The leaves are tough and low in energy, so koalas spend long hours resting between meals.

Special bacteria in a koala's gut help break down the leaves that would be too difficult for many other animals to digest.

## How do koalas live?

Koalas usually live **alone** within a home range of favorite trees. They are mostly active at night and rest during the day to save energy.

Mother koalas carry **joeys** in a backward-opening pouch, then let older joeys ride on their backs while learning to climb.

## Why are koalas at risk?

Koalas are listed as **Vulnerable**. Their forests are being cleared for roads, farms, and towns. Disease, dog attacks, and bushfires also harm wild populations.

Protecting eucalyptus habitat and wildlife corridors gives koalas safer places to feed and raise joeys.

## Life cycle and joeys

A baby koala is called a **joey**. Joeys are born tiny and crawl into the mother's pouch, where they finish growing for months.

After leaving the pouch, joeys cling to their mother's back and slowly learn which eucalyptus leaves are safe to eat.

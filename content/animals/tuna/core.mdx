## What is a tuna?

A **tuna** is a large, fast ocean fish built for speed. Yellowfin tuna have shiny blue bodies, powerful tails, and bright yellow fins that stand out in open water.

Yellowfin tuna belong to the family Scombridae. Their scientific name is *Thunnus albacares*.

## Where do tuna live?

Yellowfin tuna live in the **warm open ocean** of tropical and subtropical seas around the world. They are usually found far from shore in deep, blue water.

They follow currents and schools of prey across vast stretches of sea.

## What do tuna eat?

Tuna are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). They hunt fish, squid, and crustaceans. Their speed lets them chase fast prey like flying fish and mackerel.

Tuna often feed near the surface but can dive deep to find food.

## How do tuna live?

Tuna are **strong swimmers** that may travel in large schools. They are one of the few fish that can stay **warm-blooded**, keeping their muscles ready for fast chasing.

Dolphins, sharks, and humans are among their main threats.

## Why are tuna at risk?

Yellowfin tuna are listed as **Near Threatened**. Heavy fishing for food has reduced tuna numbers in many parts of the world.

Sustainable fishing rules and protected ocean areas can help tuna populations recover.

## Life cycle and larvae

Tuna spawn in open water, releasing millions of tiny eggs. After hatching, **larvae** drift in the ocean before growing into fast juvenile fish.

Young tuna must grow quickly to survive in the open sea.

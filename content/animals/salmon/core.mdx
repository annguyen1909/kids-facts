## What is a salmon?

A **salmon** is a fish famous for its incredible life journey. Atlantic salmon hatch in rivers, grow strong in the ocean, and many adults swim all the way home again to spawn.

Atlantic salmon belong to the family Salmonidae. Their scientific name is *Salmo salar*.

## Where do salmon live?

Atlantic salmon begin life in **cold, clean rivers** in Europe and North America. After growing in freshwater, they migrate to the **North Atlantic Ocean** to feed.

When it is time to reproduce, adults return to freshwater rivers—often the same ones where they hatched.

## What do salmon eat?

Salmon are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore). Young salmon in rivers eat insects and tiny animals. Adults in the ocean eat small fish, shrimp, and other marine prey.

Their diet changes as they move from stream to sea and back again.

## How do salmon live?

Salmon are **strong swimmers** built for long migrations. They can leap waterfalls and swim against powerful river currents.

After spawning, most Atlantic salmon die, while their eggs begin the next generation hidden in gravel nests.

## Why are salmon at risk?

Atlantic salmon are listed as **Least Concern** globally, but many wild runs have declined. Dams, pollution, warming rivers, and overfishing make life harder for salmon.

Protecting clean rivers and safe ocean habitat helps salmon complete their remarkable life cycle.

## Life cycle and fry

A very young salmon is called a **fry** or **parr**. Eggs are laid in gravel nests called redds. After hatching, juveniles grow in the river before becoming **smolts** that head to sea.

Years later, adult salmon may travel thousands of miles to return home and spawn.

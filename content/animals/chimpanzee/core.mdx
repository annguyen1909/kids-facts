## What is a chimpanzee?

A chimpanzee is a **great ape** and one of the smartest animals in the forest. Chimpanzees have long arms, opposable thumbs, and expressive faces. They can climb, use tools, and solve problems.

Chimpanzees belong to the family Hominidae. Their scientific name is *Pan troglodytes*.

## Where do chimpanzees live?

Chimpanzees live in **tropical forests and woodlands** across central and western Africa. They need trees for food, nests, and travel routes through the canopy.

Healthy forests with fruiting trees and water help chimpanzee communities survive year-round.

## What do chimpanzees eat?

Chimpanzees are **omnivores**. They eat fruit, leaves, seeds, flowers, insects, and sometimes eggs or small animals.

Fruit is often their favorite food, especially when trees are full during the rainy season.

## How do chimpanzees live?

Chimpanzees live in [social communities](/topics/social-animals) that may include dozens of members. They groom one another, share food patches, and communicate with sounds, gestures, and facial expressions.

At night, chimpanzees build **leafy nests** in trees to sleep safely above the forest floor.

## Why are chimpanzees at risk?

Chimpanzees are listed as **Endangered**. Logging, farming, and road building break up their forests. Hunting and disease also threaten wild populations.

Protected parks and forest corridors help chimpanzees move, feed, and raise infants.

## Life cycle and infants

A baby chimpanzee is called an **infant**. Infants are born after about eight months and stay close to their mothers for years.

Young chimpanzees learn by watching adults how to find food, climb, build nests, and use tools.

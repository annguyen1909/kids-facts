## What is a rhinoceros?

A **rhinoceros** is one of the largest land mammals on Earth. White rhinoceroses have thick gray skin, two horns on the nose, and a wide mouth for grazing grass.

White rhinos belong to the family Rhinocerotidae. Their scientific name is *Ceratotherium simum*.

## Where do rhinos live?

White rhinos live in **African savannas and grasslands** where they can graze on open plains. They need grass, water, and muddy wallows to cool off.

Explore more [savanna animals](/habitats/savanna) that share these open landscapes with rhinos.

## What do rhinos eat?

White rhinos are **herbivores**. They graze on grasses and other low plants, using their wide lips to crop vegetation close to the ground.

An adult white rhino may eat more than 100 pounds of grass in a day.

## How do rhinos live?

White rhinos usually move across open habitat in small groups or alone. They often visit **mud wallows** to cool their skin and discourage biting insects.

Mothers stay close to calves and guard them carefully from predators.

## Why are rhinos at risk?

White rhinos are listed as **Near Threatened**. Poaching for horns is the biggest danger, even though horns are made of keratin like fingernails.

Protected reserves and anti-poaching patrols have helped some white rhino populations recover.

## Life cycle and calves

A baby rhinoceros is called a **calf**. After a long pregnancy, a single calf is born and can stand within hours.

Calves follow their mothers across the savanna while learning where to graze and find water.

## What is a spotted hyena?

A spotted hyena is a strong, dog-like **carnivore** that lives in Africa. It has a sloping back, rounded ears, and sandy fur covered in dark **spots**. No two hyenas have exactly the same spot pattern.

Even though they look a bit like dogs, hyenas are not dogs at all. They belong to their own animal family called **Hyaenidae** and are actually closer relatives of cats and mongooses. Their scientific name is *Crocuta crocuta*.

## Where do spotted hyenas live?

Spotted hyenas live across **sub-Saharan Africa**. You can find them in savannas, grasslands, open woodlands, and even dry semi-deserts.

They like places with plenty of grazing animals to hunt and enough space to roam. A clan defends a large home area called a territory, marking the edges with scent so other hyenas know to stay away.

## What do spotted hyenas eat?

Spotted hyenas are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore), which means they eat meat. They are surprisingly good **hunters** and catch much of their own food, chasing animals such as wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle.

They are also famous **scavengers**. With jaws strong enough to crush bone, a hyena can eat parts of a carcass that other predators leave behind, so very little goes to waste.

## How do hyena clans live together?

Spotted hyenas are very **social** and live in family groups called **clans**. A clan can have up to 80 members, and it is always led by a powerful **top female**. Female hyenas are usually bigger and stronger than the males.

Clan members greet each other, hunt together, and use sounds, scents, and body language to keep the peace. Their giggling "laugh" is one of many calls they use to stay in touch.

## Life cycle and cubs

A baby hyena is called a **cub**. Cubs are born in a den with their eyes already open and a few teeth, which is unusual for baby mammals. They have dark fur that slowly grows spots as they get older.

Several mothers may share one big **communal den**. Cubs drink their mother's rich milk for many months and slowly learn the rules and ranks of clan life by watching the adults.

## Why are spotted hyenas at risk?

Spotted hyenas are listed as **Least Concern**, so they are not endangered. Still, their numbers are slowly **decreasing** in some places.

As farms and towns spread into wild areas, hyenas lose habitat and sometimes come into conflict with people. Protected parks and reserves across Africa help keep healthy clans roaming the savanna.

## Are hyenas really dogs?

No! This is one of the most common mix-ups about hyenas. Their long legs and bushy look make them seem like wild dogs, but they sit on their own branch of the animal family tree.

Hyenas, cats, mongooses, and meerkats all belong to the same big group of meat-eaters. So the next time someone calls a hyena a dog, you will know the surprising truth.

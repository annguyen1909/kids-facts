## What is a fox?

A fox is a **small wild dog** known for speed, sharp hearing, and a bushy tail. The red fox has a reddish coat, white chest, and black legs and ear tips.

Red foxes belong to the dog family Canidae. Their scientific name is *Vulpes vulpes*.

## Where do foxes live?

Red foxes are **highly adaptable**. They live in forests, grasslands, farms, deserts, mountains, and even parks on the edge of cities across Europe, Asia, North America, and parts of Africa.

Foxes often rest in thick cover during the day and become more active after sunset.

## What do foxes eat?

Foxes are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore), but they also eat plants sometimes. They hunt mice, voles, rabbits, birds, and insects, and may eat berries or fruit when prey is scarce.

A fox often hunts **alone**, listening for movement under leaves or snow before pouncing.

## How do foxes live?

Foxes usually live alone or in small family groups during the breeding season. They communicate with barks, screams, and scent marks.

A fox uses its **tail for balance** when running and curling up for warmth in cold weather.

## Why are foxes at risk?

Red foxes are listed as **Least Concern** and remain widespread. Even so, hunting, habitat loss, and car strikes can hurt local populations.

In many places, foxes have learned to live near people while still depending on wild prey and safe den sites.

## Life cycle and kits

A baby fox is called a **kit** or cub. Kits are born in spring inside an underground **den**. They are blind at first and depend on their parents for milk and protection.

Young kits play and practice pouncing before learning to hunt on their own.

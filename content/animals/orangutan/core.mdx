## What is an orangutan?

An **orangutan** is a great ape and one of the largest tree-dwelling mammals in the world. Orangutans have long reddish hair, strong hands, and arms that are longer than their legs. They are famous for moving slowly and carefully through the rainforest canopy.

Bornean orangutans belong to the family Hominidae. Their scientific name is *Pongo pygmaeus*.

## Where do orangutans live?

Bornean orangutans live only in **tropical rainforests on the island of Borneo** in Indonesia and Malaysia. They need tall trees for food, travel, and sleeping nests.

Healthy peat swamps and lowland forests give orangutans the fruit and leaves they depend on.

## What do orangutans eat?

Orangutans are mostly **herbivores**. They eat fruit, leaves, bark, flowers, and sometimes insects.

When fruit trees are fruiting, orangutans may travel through the canopy gathering ripe food high above the ground.

## How do orangutans live?

Unlike many other apes, adult orangutans usually live **alone**. Mothers are the main caregivers and stay with their infants for many years.

Each night, orangutans build a **fresh leafy nest** in the trees to sleep safely above predators.

## Why are orangutans at risk?

Bornean orangutans are listed as **Critically Endangered**. Rainforests are being cleared for palm oil plantations, logging, and farming.

Roads and fragmented forests make it harder for orangutans to find food and move safely between trees.

## Life cycle and infants

A baby orangutan is called an **infant**. Infants are born after about eight and a half months and cling tightly to their mothers.

Young orangutans learn which fruits and trees are safe by watching their mothers for years before living on their own.

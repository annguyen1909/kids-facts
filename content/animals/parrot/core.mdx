## What is a parrot?

A **parrot** is a bird with a curved beak and feet that grip like hands. The scarlet macaw is one of the biggest and brightest parrots, with red, yellow, and blue feathers.

Scarlet macaws belong to the family Psittacidae. Their scientific name is *Ara macao*.

## Where do parrots live?

Scarlet macaws live in **tropical rainforests** from southern Mexico through Central America and into the Amazon. They fly high above the trees and nest in old hollow trunks.

Healthy rainforest with large trees is essential for macaw survival.

## What do parrots eat?

Macaws eat mostly plants. They feed on **fruit, nuts, seeds, and flowers** and use their strong hooked beaks to crack hard nuts open.

They often grip food with one foot while they eat.

## How do parrots live?

Macaws are **social birds** that often travel in pairs or small groups. They call loudly to stay in contact across the forest.

Pairs may use the same nest hole year after year.

## Why are parrots at risk?

Scarlet macaws are listed as **Least Concern** globally, but forest clearing and illegal capture for the pet trade have hurt wild populations in some areas.

Protecting old nest trees and rainforest helps macaws thrive.

## Life cycle and chicks

Macaws lay eggs in tree cavities high above the ground. **Chicks** hatch after about a month and depend on both parents for food.

Young macaws stay with their parents for months before flying off on their own.

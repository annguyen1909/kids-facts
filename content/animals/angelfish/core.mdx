## What is an angelfish?

An **angelfish** is a bright reef fish with elegant fins and bold colors. The emperor angelfish has striking blue and yellow stripes that make it one of the most beautiful fish on coral reefs.

Emperor angelfish belong to the family Pomacanthidae. Their scientific name is *Pomacanthus imperator*.

## Where do angelfish live?

Emperor angelfish live on **coral reefs** in warm, clear ocean water. Their range stretches from the **Red Sea and East Africa** across the Indian Ocean to **Hawaii and Polynesia**.

Healthy reefs with sponges and algae give them food and places to hide.

## What do angelfish eat?

Angelfish are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore) that also graze on algae. They eat sponges, algae, and small invertebrates on the reef. Their sharp teeth help them nip food from coral and rock.

Adults often graze slowly across their home territory.

## How do angelfish live?

Angelfish are **territorial reef fish** that defend a patch of coral. Adults may stay with a partner and patrol the same area for years.

Their bright colors may warn rivals while juveniles use different patterns to hide.

## Why are angelfish at risk?

Emperor angelfish are listed as **Least Concern**, but they depend on healthy coral reefs. Reef damage, warming oceans, and collection for aquariums can hurt local populations.

Protecting reefs helps angelfish, clownfish, and countless reef species.

## Life cycle and larvae

Young angelfish hatch as tiny **larvae** drifting in open water. Juveniles have dark bodies with white rings, very different from striped adults.

It can take years for a young angelfish to develop its full adult colors.

## What is a red panda?

A red panda is a small, tree-climbing mammal from the mountains of Asia. It has soft reddish-brown fur, a bushy ringed tail, and white markings on its face. Red pandas are about the size of a house cat, but their long tail makes them look bigger.

Red pandas belong to their own family, **Ailuridae**. They are **not** bears, and they are not closely related to giant pandas even though both eat bamboo. Their scientific name is *Ailurus fulgens*.

## Where do red pandas live?

Red pandas live in cool, rainy **mountain forests** in the eastern Himalayas and nearby parts of China. Countries such as Nepal, India, Bhutan, and Myanmar have wild red panda populations.

These forests have bamboo growing beneath the trees. Red pandas use branches for resting, traveling, and escaping predators such as snow leopards.

## What do red pandas eat?

Red pandas are **omnivores**, but bamboo is their main food. They eat bamboo leaves and shoots for many hours each day. They also eat fruit, berries, eggs, and insects when they can find them.

A special wrist bone works like a **false thumb**, helping red pandas grip bamboo while they feed.

## How do red pandas behave?

Most adult red pandas live **alone**. They are excellent climbers and spend much of their time in trees. They are most active at dawn and dusk, when they look for food and move through the forest.

Mother red pandas raise **cubs** in a nest lined with leaves and fur. Cubs learn to climb trees as they grow.

## Why are red pandas at risk?

Red pandas are listed as **Endangered**. Their forest homes are being cut down, broken into smaller pieces, and disturbed by roads and farming. They are also threatened by hunting and by dogs that spread disease.

Protected forests and community conservation programs help give red pandas safer places to live.

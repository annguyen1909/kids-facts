## Are red pandas carnivores or herbivores?

Red pandas are **omnivores**—animals that can eat both plants and animals. In the wild, however, they behave like **bamboo specialists**. Most of their day is spent finding and chewing bamboo.

Even though red pandas belong to the order Carnivora (the same group as cats and dogs), their diet is mostly plant-based. Their digestive system is adapted for bamboo rather than large prey.

## What is bamboo?

**Bamboo** is a fast-growing plant with hollow stems and tough leaves. In red panda habitat, bamboo grows thickly on the forest floor and on mountain slopes. Red pandas rely on it because it is available year-round in many forests.

They prefer tender **shoots** and fresh **leaves**, which are easier to chew and digest than older, woody stems.

## How do red pandas eat bamboo?

Red pandas use sharp teeth to slice through leaves and shoots. Their most famous feeding tool is the **false thumb**—an extended wrist bone that helps them grasp bamboo like a thumb and finger.

This adaptation lets a red panda:

- Hold a bamboo stem steady
- Strip leaves efficiently
- Feed while sitting on a branch

## What else do red pandas eat?

When bamboo is scarce or seasons change, red pandas broaden their diet. They may eat:

- **Berries and fruit**
- **Acorns and roots**
- **Bird eggs**
- **Insects and small animals**

This flexibility helps them survive in patchy mountain habitats.

## Why does diet matter for conservation?

If bamboo forests are cut down or broken up by roads, red pandas lose their main food source. Protecting whole forest areas—not just single trees—helps red pandas find enough bamboo every day.

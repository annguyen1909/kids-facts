## What is a red panda habitat?

A **habitat** is the natural home where an animal finds food, shelter, water, and space to raise young. For red pandas, the best habitats are **cool, moist mountain forests** with plenty of bamboo and tall trees.

These forests are often called **temperate broadleaf and conifer forests**. They grow on mountain slopes where rainfall is high and temperatures stay mild—not tropical hot, but not freezing all year either.

## Where do red pandas live in the wild?

Red pandas are found across the **eastern Himalayas** and into southwestern China. Important range countries include:

- **Nepal** — forests such as Langtang National Park
- **India** — Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and Darjeeling hills
- **Bhutan** — forested mountain valleys
- **China** — Yunnan and Sichuan provinces
- **Myanmar** — northern forest regions

Within these countries, red pandas use **connected forest patches** where bamboo and tree cover overlap.

## Why do red pandas need bamboo forests?

Bamboo is the red panda's main food. A good habitat must include:

- **Bamboo understory** for daily feeding
- **Tall trees** for climbing and resting
- **Tree hollows and forks** for sleeping
- **Clean water** from streams and rainfall

If bamboo is removed by logging or farming, red pandas cannot stay in that area even if trees remain.

## What is the climate like?

Red panda habitats are usually:

- **Cool** rather than hot
- **Rainy or misty** for much of the year
- **Seasonal**, with colder winters at higher elevations

Thick fur helps red pandas stay warm. In warmer months, they move to shadier forest areas when they can.

## Why is habitat loss a problem?

Forests are being cleared for farms, roads, and towns. When habitat is broken into small pieces, red pandas struggle to:

- Find enough bamboo
- Meet mates safely
- Escape predators
- Move between forest patches

Protected parks and wildlife corridors help connect remaining forests so red pandas can survive long term.

## What is a Tokay gecko?

The Tokay gecko is a large nocturnal lizard belonging to the gecko family Gekkonidae. It is famous for its **distinctive appearance: a blue-grey body covered in bright orange-red spots**. They have a wide head, large golden eyes with vertical slit pupils, and a muscular body.

Their scientific name is *Gekko gecko*. They are some of the largest geckos in the world. They are best known for their vocal abilities and their gravity-defying climbing skills.

## Where do Tokay geckos live?

Tokay geckos are native to **South and Southeast Asia**, ranging from northeast India and Bangladesh to southern China, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. They live in **tropical rainforests, rocky cliffs, and trees**.

However, they are also very common in **urban settings**. They often enter houses and hang on ceilings or walls near lights, where they hunt insects. They have also been introduced to warm regions outside Asia, such as Florida in the United States.

## What do Tokay geckos eat?

Tokay geckos are **active carnivores**. Because they hunt at night (nocturnal), they look for prey under the cover of darkness. Their diet consists of:
- **Insects and Spiders**: They eat crickets, grasshoppers, moths, beetles, cockroaches, and spiders.
- **Small Animals**: Large adult geckos will occasionally catch and eat small mice, frogs, small birds, and other small lizards.

They have very strong jaws and sharp teeth, allowing them to crush the tough exoskeletons of large beetles and hold onto struggling prey.

## How do Tokay geckos behave?

Tokay geckos are solitary, aggressive, and **highly territorial** lizards. Males communicate with other geckos by making a loud, repetitive barking sound that sounds exactly like **\"To-kay! To-kay!\"**. They use this call to defend their home area and warn competitors away.

They are also famous climbing experts. Their toes are covered in millions of microscopic hairs called **setae** that create molecular stickiness, allowing them to run upside down across glass ceilings and vertical walls.

## Life cycle and babies

Tokay geckos are solitary lizards that only gather to breed. Females lay **two hard-shelled eggs** in a hidden spot, such as under tree bark or inside a wall crevice, and glue them to the surface.

Unlike most lizards, Tokay geckos are protective parents. The male and female often guard the eggs until they hatch. After **3 to 4 months**, the baby geckos (hatchlings) break out of the eggs. They are about 3 inches long and are immediately ready to hunt insects.

## Why are Tokay geckos at risk?

Tokay geckos are currently listed as Least Concern, but they face threats from **habitat loss** as rainforests are cleared, and heavy **poaching for the pet trade and traditional medicine**.

In many parts of Asia, geckos are captured and dried to be used in traditional remedies, despite no scientific evidence that they cure illnesses. Conservation groups work to regulate the trade and protect their tropical forest habitats.

## What is a flamingo?

A **flamingo** is a tall wading bird with pink feathers, stilt-like legs, and a bent beak. American flamingos live in warm coastal wetlands and are famous for standing on one leg.

American flamingos belong to the family Phoenicopteridae. Their scientific name is *Phoenicopterus ruber*.

## Where do flamingos live?

American flamingos live in **shallow lagoons and coastal wetlands** in the Caribbean, Galápagos Islands, and northern South America. They wade in salty or muddy water where few other birds feed.

Huge flocks gather on islands and mudflats to nest.

## What do flamingos eat?

Flamingos are [carnivores](/diets/carnivore) that eat tiny animals. They filter **brine shrimp, algae, and small crustaceans** from the water with their specialized beaks.

Their food gives them the pigments that turn their feathers pink.

## How do flamingos live?

Flamingos are **social birds** that live in large colonies. They build mud nests, lay one egg, and both parents feed the chick with a special crop milk.

They often stand on one leg, which may help them stay warm.

## Why are flamingos at risk?

American flamingos are listed as **Least Concern**, but they need clean wetlands and safe nesting islands. Pollution, draining of lagoons, and disturbance can harm colonies.

Protecting coastal wetlands helps flamingos and many other water birds.

## Life cycle and chicks

Flamingo **chicks** hatch with gray fluffy down, not pink feathers. They join groups called crèches while parents take turns feeding them.

As chicks grow and eat more shrimp and algae, their feathers slowly turn pink.

## Why giant panda behavior is special

Giant pandas are one of the few large mammals that spend most of their lives **eating low-calorie bamboo** while living alone in mountain forests. They combine **slow movement**, **solitary habits**, and **quiet communication** to survive.

Unlike wolves or dolphins, giant pandas do not live in large social groups. Most adults keep their own home range and meet others mainly during breeding season.

## Do giant pandas live alone?

Yes. **Adult giant pandas are mostly solitary.** Each animal uses a home area with enough bamboo and trees to survive. They mark territory with scent from glands near the tail and by scratching trees.

Solitary living reduces competition for food in forests where bamboo may be patchy.

## When are giant pandas active?

Giant pandas do not follow a strict day-or-night schedule. They **feed for many hours**, then **rest** before feeding again. A typical day includes:

- Searching for bamboo shoots and leaves
- Sitting and chewing bamboo for hours
- Resting in shade or in a tree
- Moving to a new feeding patch

Because bamboo is low in energy, pandas save strength by moving slowly on the ground.

## How do giant pandas use trees?

Trees are important to giant panda life. Pandas use branches to:

- **Rest** safely off the ground
- **Escape** from threats when possible
- **Reach** bamboo in higher branches
- **Watch** their surroundings from above

Strong claws and flexible limbs make giant pandas capable climbers, even though adults are heavy.

## How do mothers raise cubs?

Mother giant pandas usually give birth to one or two **cubs**, though often only one survives. Cubs are born tiny, blind, and pink. The mother keeps them warm in a den—often a hollow tree or rocky crevice.

As cubs grow, they learn to:

- Grip bamboo with their pseudo-thumb
- Climb short distances near the den
- Recognize their mother's scent and calls

Young giant pandas stay with their mother for about **18 months to two years** before living on their own.

## How do giant pandas communicate?

Giant pandas are usually quiet, but they communicate in several ways:

- **Scent marking** on trees, rocks, and trails
- **Bleats and chirps** during breeding season
- **Paw scratches** on tree trunks
- **Gentle play** between mothers and cubs

These behaviors help giant pandas share information without drawing too much attention.

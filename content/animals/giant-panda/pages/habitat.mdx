## What is a giant panda habitat?

A **habitat** is the natural home where an animal finds food, shelter, water, and space to raise young. For giant pandas, the best habitats are **cool, moist mountain forests** with plenty of bamboo.

These forests grow on mountain slopes in central China, where rainfall is high and temperatures stay mild—not tropical hot, but not freezing all year either.

## Where do giant pandas live in the wild?

Giant pandas are found only in **central China**. Their wild range is split into several mountain regions:

- **Sichuan province** — including Wolong, Minshan, and Qionglai mountain forests
- **Shaanxi province** — Qinling Mountains (home to the Qinling panda subpopulation)
- **Gansu province** — small forest areas near the Minshan range

Within these regions, pandas use **connected forest patches** where bamboo and tree cover overlap.

## Why do giant pandas need bamboo forests?

Bamboo is the giant panda's main food. A good habitat must include:

- **Bamboo understory** for daily feeding
- **Tall trees** for climbing and resting
- **Streams and rainfall** for clean water
- **Quiet, undisturbed slopes** for raising cubs

If bamboo is removed by logging or farming, giant pandas cannot stay in that area even if trees remain.

## What is the climate like?

Giant panda habitats are usually:

- **Cool and misty** for much of the year
- **Rainy**, especially in summer
- **Seasonal**, with snow at higher elevations in winter

Thick fur helps giant pandas stay warm. In warmer months, they may move to shadier forest areas at higher elevations.

## Why is habitat loss a problem?

Forests have been cleared for farms, roads, and towns. When habitat is broken into small pieces, giant pandas struggle to:

- Find enough bamboo
- Meet mates safely
- Move between forest patches
- Raise cubs away from disturbance

Protected reserves and **wildlife corridors** help connect remaining forests so giant pandas can survive long term.
